## Part 3: Data privacy & security

---

# Data privacy & security

- The default for many LLM interfaces is to record and store your data and interactions for future training

- Risk of leaking personally identifiable information or sensitive research data

- GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation, EU)
  - People have control over their personal data and organisations are accountable for how they collect, use and store it
  - Applies even if outside the EU if it is personal data from those within EU
  - Servers could be in a different jurisdiction when you interact with an LLM

---

# Data privacy & security

- Possible solutions:
  - Local LLMs (run on HPC)
  - Enterprise accounts with data agreements
  - Don't send data! (e.g. send schemas instead of data or anonymise data)
  - Ensure connections are encrypted
  - When using Agents, ensure they are only granted access to data that is strictly needed
